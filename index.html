To find out what's inside your PC, you can use built-in tools like msinfo32 or Device Manager in Windows, which provide details about your hardware components. Third-party software like HWInfo, and Speccy offer more comprehensive information. Additionally, you can open your PC case and visually inspect the components, such as the CPU, RAM, motherboard, and GPU. Ensure your PC is turned off and unplugged before opening it. Labels on the components can help identify them. This combined approach gives you a clear understanding of the parts in your PC.
